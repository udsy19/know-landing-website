- Need to send **306 cold emails** to get ONE lead

**For VCs & Investors:**
- 64% of deal flow is cold (low quality)
- Cold contacts: **1.19% chance** of reaching investment committee
- Warm intros: **26% chance** of reaching investment committee
- **22x higher conversion** with warm introductions

**For Recruiters:**
- **70% of workforce** is passive (not actively looking)
- Generic InMails get **5-8%** response
- Personalized outreach gets **15-25%** response
- **52% of hiring managers** say passive recruiting has grown less effective

### The Core Problem:

> "Everyone has a network. Nobody knows how to use it."

Your best path to anyone is buried in your email, calendar, and LinkedIn—but you can't see it. You don't know who knows who, how strong those relationships are, or who can actually make an introduction.

**The opportunity**: Transform every professional's dormant network into an active, searchable, actionable asset.

# SLIDE 4: UNDERLYING MAGIC (Technology)

## The Relationship Graph + AI Research Engine

### How It Works:

**1. Connect Your Network (Private)**
```
Gmail / Outlook → Email patterns, frequency, recency
Calendar → Meeting history, face-time
LinkedIn → Connection graph, shared connections
```

**2. Build the Relationship Graph**
- Proprietary **Relationship Strength Algorithm (0.0-1.0)**
  - Email frequency & recency
  - Meeting count & duration
  - Response rates & time-to-respond
  - LinkedIn interaction signals
  - Bi-directional analysis (do they respond to you?)

**3. Extend to 2nd & 3rd Degree**
- Map connections-of-connections
- Calculate **path strength** (weakest link in chain)
- Surface hidden paths you didn't know existed

**4. AI-Powered People Research**
- 50+ public data sources aggregated:
  - LinkedIn, Twitter/X, company websites
  - Crunchbase, AngelList, PitchBook
  - News articles, press releases, podcasts
  - SEC filings, patents, academic papers
  - Conference talks, YouTube interviews
- Natural language synthesis into actionable briefs
- Real-time updates when new information surfaces

## Design Notes for Slides

### Visual Style:
- **Colors**: Dark background, white text, single accent color
- **Typography**: Clean sans-serif, 30pt+ body, 50pt+ headlines
- **Imagery**: Minimal, product screenshots, data visualizations
- **Icons**: Simple line icons for concepts

### Per-Slide Guidelines:

1. **Title**: Logo large, tagline underneath, minimal
2. **Problem**: Big stat callouts, pain points as bullets
3. **Value Prop**: Single powerful sentence, before/after comparison
4. **Technology**: Simplified diagram, avoid jargon
5. **Business Model**: Pricing table, unit economics
6. **GTM**: Funnel visual, channel breakdown
7. **Competition**: 2x2 matrix positioning chart
8. **Team**: Photos, 2-3 bullets each, no fluff
9. **Financials**: Hockey stick chart, key metrics table
10. **Ask**: Clear number, milestone timeline, urgency

### Presentation Tips:

- Lead with the problem, not your solution
- Use specific numbers, not vague claims
- Tell a story: Problem → Solution → Why Now → Why Us
- Have backup slides ready for deep-dive questions
- Practice the 20-minute version AND a 5-minute version
